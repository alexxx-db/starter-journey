Create Workspaces

An extended explanation can be found in: Functional Workspace Organization on Databricks

Journey progress​

  • Identify your organization cloud tenant(s).
  • Create the workspace(s).
  • Post-deployment configurations.
  • Governance Strategy on Unity Catalog.

Target​

  • This section shows how to create the first Databricks Workspace (development) on the previously identified cloud tenant.
  • Repeat the Terraform or manual method for the next workspaces (staging, uat, qa, prod, etc...).

Outcome​

  • Databricks Workspace (development workspace).
  • UC catalog (development catalog).
  • Cloud infra.

Three workspaces: Best practice and industry standard.​

Recommended number of workspaces
  • Three workspaces.
  • Aligned with the SDLC (dev, staging, uat, production).
    • Staging is often named as QA or testing depending on the organization context.
    • Treat your data and AI as you treat your code.

Three workspaces per business unit or department​

If three workspaces is not enought to separate your organization teams and business units do the following:

Business unit and teams separation.
  • Keep the three workspaces idea.
  • Add the (business unit | line of business | team name) as a prefix to the workspace names.
  • For each (business unit | line of business | team name): create a dev - staging - prod workspace.

A single workspace to manage everything​

One place for all the jobs, pipelines, dashboards and AI models in a organization.
  • Dev, Staging and Prod workloads on a single workspace is highly prone to errors.
  • All users and automations service principals working and deploying on a single environment.
  • This is the GIT equivalent of working only on the main branch.
  • Treat your data and AI as you treat your code.
